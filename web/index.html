Operational bottlenecks

  • Vendor/medical — route, don’t sell; they eat handle time.
  • Peak windows: 11–12, 12–1, 1–2 local — staff and overflow here first.

Immediate revenue actions

  • Cut CAC: negatives + query sculpting for non-case and wrong-firm traffic.
  • Lift volume: reduce abandonment in peak hours (schedule + overflow).
  • Force disposition + tag on every call — eliminate blind Not Scored.

Final executive summary

This line is not a marketing funnel — it is a demand blender. Out of 543 calls, 15.84% present as true PI opportunity under a conservative model. Roughly 47.51% is non-actionable acquisition noise (vendor, wrong firm, PD/non-case), before existing client load (10.68%) and intake failure (11.97%). If you buy clicks against general legal intent and brand confusion, you tax CAC with calls that won’t become cases. Mandate: separate acquisition from client service and vendor traffic, staff peak hours, close the loop on Not Scored. Growth is quality + speed, not only more spend.

Action playbook

Intake script

  • 3-second opener: firm + PI + geography; branch existing clients to client-care.
  • Mandatory capture: name, date, injuries, EMS, fault, insurance — before story.
  • Hang-up save: one closing question after silent/dead air.

Routing

  • VIP: Spanish + injury vs PD-only; vendor → admin or auto-attendant.
  • After-hours aligned to peaks; overflow for top sources.

Marketing

  • Negatives: employment, tax, family, criminal, competitor names in search terms.
  • Campaign negatives for wrong-firm/misdial clusters; tighten geo where misdials cluster.

Brand

  • Distinct numbers: brand vs performance; clear verbal ID in ads.
  • LSA/Maps: firm name + injury, not generic “lawyers.”

Tracking

  • 100% disposition — no chronic Not Scored.
  • UTM + GCLID; CallRail aligned to Google Ads structure.
  • Weekly: Qualified Lead vs CRM signed.

Classification: rule-based priority stack — spot-check UNTAGGED_OR_AMBIGUOUS rows.
