Definitions (plain English)

Qualified lead rate — What CallRail shows as “Qualified Lead” vs everything else.

True PI (modeled) — Our best read from tags + status for injury-type opportunity. Not the same as CallRail’s button.

Intake failure / dead-air bucket — Hang-ups, abandoned calls, “silent” or dead-air tags, or super-short connects with no real intake. These are usually handling or connect issues, not “bad marketing” by themselves.

  • Call volume: About 538 calls in the first 20 days of March vs 380 across February’s export — daily pace is higher in March.
  • Qualified rate: CallRail “Qualified Lead” rate went from 4.5% to 12.1%.

  • Google/Meta vs Direct/GMB: PPC-heavy (Google Ads + Meta) 98 → 239; Direct + GMB 134 → 188.

Plain read: If rings go up but “Not Scored” stays huge, you’re busy — not necessarily winning.

Projection commentary (March month-end)

  • Projected March calls (excl. tests): ~834 if the first 20 days represent the whole month.
  • Caveat: Pace can overstate if the last third of March is slower (weekends, budget caps) or understate if you run heavy weekend LSA/PPC.
  • If nothing changes: higher volume with weak tagging = marketing looks “fine” while intake quality stays invisible.
  • Fast win: cut dead-air + force disposition on “Not Scored” — that alone tightens forecasting.

Operational notes

  • Dead-air and abandon calls are often fixable with answer speed, IVR, and staffing on the heatmap peaks.
  • Attorney-switch and “messy” injury stories need a calm triage script — not instant disqualify.

Recommended actions

Intake (priority)

  • End every call with a disposition — “Not Scored” should be rare, not normal.
  • 10-second opener: firm name, PI, area — then route existing clients before they burn new-lead time.
  • Save hang-ups: one last question before you let silent calls drop.
  • Train on attorney-switch language — don’t lose those to speed.

Ops / routing

  • Match headcount to the heatmap (late morning / early afternoon Pacific).
  • Send vendor/medical to admin lines.

Marketing / PPC

  • Pull search terms / LSA queries weekly; add negatives for obvious non-PI.

Reporting

  • Reconcile CallRail qualified vs signed cases monthly.

Bottom line

You probably don’t need more raw calls as much as cleaner receipt: answer discipline, tagging, and triage. Most of the waste here is preventable — calls that never get scored, or fail before a real conversation. Fixing that usually beats another point of bid.
