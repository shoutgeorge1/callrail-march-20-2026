February vs March 2026 — Intake & Call Quality

March figures are through March 20, 2026. February reflects the export window in file (starts Feb 2 in this dataset). March month-end projection scales the first 20 days to 31 days — illustrative only.

This report was produced with assistance from Cursor AI. Figures and modeled categories may contain errors and should be validated before operational use. February labeling is uneven in places; March is partial-month; projections use simple pacing.

Tags and modeled buckets summarize call content; they do not replace a formal intake QA program or a legal determination of a “lead.”

Only calls marked “Qualified Lead” carry value-back scoring to Google. That marking reflects operator judgment from listening to recordings — not an independent measure of lead quality. Other calls remain in the default CallRail disposition until explicitly marked.

Definitions

Qualified Lead (value-back) — Calls marked in CallRail for Google value-back. In this workflow, value and scoring are applied only there, based on operator judgment from listening to recordings.

True PI (modeled) — A coarse category from tags and status for reporting; not equivalent to a retained case or a firm’s intake definition.

Default disposition (unmarked) — Calls without a value-back mark; neutral administrative state.

Silent / abandon / connect (modeled) — Hang-ups, abandoned rings, silent or dead-air tags, or very short connects — grouped for volume analysis, not as a critique of intake staff.

Recommendations — general practices (PI scaling)

These are common practices for growing personal injury firms; they should be adapted to the firm’s operating model and resources.

Intake & disposition

  • Keep a clear rule for when a call receives a value-back mark versus remaining in the default disposition.
  • Use a short, consistent opening (firm name, practice focus, geography) before routing callers.
  • Document triage paths for common non-case or referral scenarios so the process scales with volume.

Operations & routing

  • Align coverage with peak hours from the heatmap; add overflow or backup coverage when volume grows.
  • Separate vendor, medical, or administrative lines where volume warrants it.

Marketing & PPC

  • Review search terms and LSA queries on a cadence that matches spend; refresh negative keywords as new patterns appear.
  • Compare source mix month to month before changing bids — quality shifts often show up in source mix first.

Reporting & QA

  • Periodically align CallRail marked-qualified counts with firm intake or CRM outcomes for calibration.
  • Sample recorded calls when tag categories shift materially.

Summary

The comparison is intended as a neutral snapshot: volume and source mix, selective value-back marking, and a coarse tag-based view of call themes. March shows higher daily pace than the February export window; the appropriate response is firm-specific. Operational improvements (routing, coverage, triage consistency) often scale alongside media spend.
